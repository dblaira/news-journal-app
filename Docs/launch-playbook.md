# Launch Playbook

*Operational sequence for the soft launch: first 100 invited with 3-month coupons, paywall live for everyone else. Five-week countdown.*

*Every asset in this playbook quotes the [spine](../marketing/spine.md) and [brand](../marketing/brand.md) docs verbatim. Channel shaping is length and rhythm only. No message drift.*

### Scheduling tool
Buffer or Typefully. All 10 posts queued by Sunday night before launch week. Video scripts recorded in one Saturday session, edited Sunday.

### Non-negotiables
- No hashtags except `#ontology` on the last tweet of a thread.
- No "launch day" language. "Launch" is internal vocabulary. External copy says *the room is open* or *the preview is live.*
- No countdown posts. The spine is the content; hype is not.
- No DMs to strangers. Only reply DMs to inbound questions.

## The 100-Invitee Notion Tracker

*One Notion database. Source of truth for the first-100 outreach.*

### Schema

| Field | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Name | Title | First + last |
| Channel | Select | X / LinkedIn / Personal / Referral / Inbound |
| Relationship | Select | Close / Warm / Cold / Inbound |
| Invited on | Date | When you sent the personal note |
| Replied | Checkbox | |
| Signed up | Checkbox | |
| Coupon applied | Checkbox | |
| First entry recorded | Checkbox | Week 1 signal |
| Week 2 active | Checkbox | Primary retention signal |
| Office hours attended | Number | Count |
| Notes | Text | What they said / what they need |

### The sequence per invitee
1. **Day 0 — Personal note.** 3 sentences max. Not a pitch. Tell them the axiom most likely to land with them, and that you want them in the first-100 room.
2. **Day 3 — Follow-up** if no reply. One line: *"No pressure, just making sure this didn't get buried."*
3. **Day 7 — Close.** If still no reply, do not push further. Add `declined` tag. They can come through the normal funnel later.
4. **Day of signup — Welcome in cohort channel.** The [first-100 kickoff message](./cs-playbook.md#the-kickoff-message) is pinned; no one needs a personal welcome note.

### Target composition
- **40** close-network (people Adam has worked with or learned from directly).
- **30** warm-network (people one degree out; mutual introductions).
- **20** public-inbound (replied to Email 2 with *in* OR DMed within launch week).
- **10** wildcard (someone Adam wants in the room for strategic reasons).

### Disqualifying criteria
- Anyone who would use the room as a support line rather than co-builder.
- Anyone whose ontology Adam cannot imagine being useful to at least one other person in the room.
- Anyone who asked for a discount first. The room is an offering, not a negotiation.

## Success Criteria (end of Week 2)

- **≥100 paid signups** (either monthly or annual), OR **≥75 with a clear path to 100** in the following week.
- **≥50 active first-100 users** (first entry recorded within 48h of signup).
- **≥80% retention from Week 1 to Week 2** of signed-up users.
- **P0 incident count: 0** in the first 48h. (Prep for this is the single most important pre-launch task.)
- **Friction log ≥10 entries** in launch week — not a bug; a signal that Adam is using the product like a user.

If all five land, the paywall stays on and the product moves from launch mode to operating mode.

If any single one misses, the retro decides whether the gap is product, messaging, or service — and which pillar gets the next week's attention.

## Post-Launch — Week 3 Onwards

- Move to the weekly cadence defined in [cs-playbook.md](./cs-playbook.md): 1 cohort post/week, weekly office hours, daily friction log.
- Shift 50% of social output from seed posts to *replied-to-reader* content: pick one user-surfaced pattern per week and write the next-level essay from it.
- Start the [cohort experiment](../marketing/futures.md#1--cohort--build-your-ontology-4-week-live-group) if launch-week momentum justifies it.
