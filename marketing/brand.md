# The Brand — Understood.app

*Source-of-truth positioning. Every pillar quotes from this page. No rewording per channel.*

---

## Name

**Understood.app** — the promise is in the past tense. By the time you finish a recording session, you have been understood. The product is not a tool for understanding; it is the state of being understood, made durable.

## The One-Liner

**The software that learns the causal shape of your life and tells you what to do next based on what has already worked for you, specifically.**

---

## Product — The Solution We Offer

Three outputs, ranked by what the user feels first:

1. **Dependable predictions** about what is about to happen in your life, based on what you just did. Not horoscope predictions — confidence-scored predictions derived from your own record.
2. **Real recommendations** for what to do next. Not advice. Recommendations, plural and specific, with the axiom each one rests on.
3. **A personal history that improves every day.** The record compounds. Every new entry tightens the axioms that power the first two.

Plain-language promise: *know which lever, pressed today, moves which outcome next week.*

---

## Service — The Problem We Solve

Two mechanisms, always named together:

1. **A causal knowledge graph of axioms and life domains.** An axiom is an "if → then" statement with a confidence score — the kind of thing that would still be true if you ignored it. Axioms are discovered from your record, not declared. Life domains are a fixed vocabulary of thirteen (Exercise, Sleep, Nutrition, Ambition, Health, Work, Social, Learning, Purchase, Belief, Affect, Insight, Entertainment) so the graph has consistent nodes. This is the same family of models AI research uses to decide whether one category actually causes another — we make it personal.

2. **Twenty-four hour access to an AI assistant that knows your ontology.** The assistant reads every question through the axioms you have already discovered. Ask it anything — "should I push or rest this week?", "why did last month feel off?", "what should I tell my coach?" — and the answer is grounded in your record, not the internet's average.

The problem being solved: ambitious people have more data about themselves than anyone has ever had, and almost no vocabulary to act on it. The ontology is the vocabulary.

## Market — Who Should Buy

**The modern, ambitious person who feels latent potential slipping through the cracks — the difference between incredible success and moderate achievement — and is done waiting.**

They feel one step below where they know they should be. They have tried the journal, the tracker, the coach, the planner. They have data; they do not have signal. They want the lights on.

Negative space — *who this is not for:*
- Anyone looking for emotional exploration or mood journaling. The word "journal" is not in the product.
- Anyone who wants software to tell them who to be. The ontology surfaces *their own* truths.
- Anyone looking for a free app. The price is intentional; the commitment is the mechanism.

## Price — How Much The Solution Is Worth

Three tiers. Paid from day one.

| Tier | Price | Who it is for |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **1-week preview** | Free, full access | Anyone who wants to feel the ontology working before paying |
| **Monthly** | **$100 / month** | People who cycle intensity — commit fully when it matters, step back when it doesn't |

## Voice

- **Specific, not mystical.** The electric feeling is earned by real numbers: 0.67, 0.57, 0.60, 0.95. Never by adjectives.
- **Past-tense ambition.** "Done waiting," "by the time you finish," "lights on." The reader is already mid-move.
- **Recommendations, not advice.** Advice is unrequested. Recommendations are what you pay for.
- **Record, not journal.** The verb is *record.* The noun is *your record.* The weekly summary is the *Weekly debrief.* Journal is banned in UI and copy.
- **Axiom, not rule.** The word "rule" is acceptable in UI when confidence must be conveyed to a non-technical reader, but in long-form copy we use *axiom.*
- **Ontology, not graph.** Internal engineering uses "knowledge graph." External copy uses "personal ontology."

## Anti-Positioning (what we are *not*)

- Not a journaling app (wrong vocabulary, wrong persona).
- Not a habit tracker (habits are inputs; we model outputs).
- Not a therapy adjunct (we do not interpret feelings; we correlate behavior).
- Not a coach (we do not tell you who to be; we show you who you already are).
- Not an analytics dashboard (numbers are a side effect of the ontology, not the point).
- Not a content platform (no social graph, no followers, no feed).

## Proof Anchors (rotating, always specific)

Every piece of copy should carry at least one of these specific numbers from the founder's own record:

- 19 months of MyFitnessPal data
- 4 years of Apple Watch sleep data
- 4 seed axioms discovered, confidences 0.57 / 0.60 / 0.67 / 0.95
- 13 life domains in the fixed vocabulary
- $100/month or $999/year

Never use vague proof ("lots of data," "many users," "significant improvement"). If there is no number, do not make the claim.
