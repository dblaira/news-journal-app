# Futures — What This Could Become

*A living capture doc, not a roadmap. One section per possible expansion. Each section has (a) a one-paragraph thesis, (b) the cheapest next experiment, and (c) the trigger that graduates it out of this file and into the plan.*

*Rule:* nothing in this file appears on the site, the deck, or any marketing asset until the experiment has graduated. This is Priority #3 (Speculation / Maven). It stays here until a specific experiment earns a lift-or-leverage case.

---

## 1. Cohort — Build Your Ontology (4-week live group)

**Thesis.** The single highest-velocity path to proving the method is not a blog post or a video; it is a room of ambitious people building their ontologies together over four weeks. Week 1: record. Week 2: tag. Week 3: discover first axiom. Week 4: test a recommendation against reality. The cohort makes the abstract concrete, turns Understood.app users into co-marketers, and produces 20–30 case studies per run.

**Cheapest next experiment.** One cohort of 12 people, $1,000 each, run personally. Zero tech build — Zoom + the app + a shared Notion. Goal is one filmed graduation session where each person says, out loud, the axiom they discovered.

**Graduation trigger.** One cohort completes with ≥8 participants reporting a specific axiom that changed a decision they made in the following month. When that happens, build the productized version.

## 2. Speaking Reel — The Deck On Stage

**Thesis.** The deck at `/story/deck` is already a keynote. Delivered once in a room of the right people, it becomes the single most efficient customer-acquisition channel this product will ever have — because the target market self-identifies by showing up to rooms where words like "ambition," "vocabulary," and "agency" are on the program.

**Cheapest next experiment.** Deliver the deck three times — one at a friendly private event, one at a small conference (South Park Commons, On Deck, a Summit track), one at an industry event (health tech, founder community). Film all three. Cut one 90-second reel.

**Graduation trigger.** One of those three talks produces ≥5 inbound signups from attendees within 48 hours. At that point, the reel becomes the top-of-funnel and speaking becomes a pillar.

## 3. Book / Long-Form — The Spine Expanded

**Thesis.** The spine is 1,500 words. The full version is 50,000. A book is not a marketing artifact — it is a one-time commitment that converts the spine from *a founder's story* to *a framework other people can teach.* Once the framework exists as a book, every other asset becomes a reference to a chapter, and the product becomes the tooling for the book's method.

**Cheapest next experiment.** Write the long-form version of Chapter 1 (The Vocabulary Gap) — ~8,000 words. Publish it as a single post. Measure read-to-signup conversion over 30 days.

**Graduation trigger.** Chapter 1 produces ≥50 signups and ≥3 inbound requests to publish the rest. That is the signal that a book contract (or a self-published edition) is worth the quarter it costs to write.

## 4. Productized Ontology Template — The Starter Pack

**Thesis.** Once the first 100 users have built ontologies, patterns will repeat. The Exercise×Sleep axiom will show up in many users' records. The Learning Master Key will likely replicate. A starter pack — "install these 5 candidate axioms, test them against your own record over 4 weeks" — lets new users skip the cold-start and get a signal by week one instead of week four. It is the difference between Understood.app and *Understood.app with training wheels on for the first month.*

**Cheapest next experiment.** Once 20 users have built personal ontologies, extract the most-replicated candidate axioms. Bundle them as "Starter Pack v0" inside the app. A/B test: new users with Starter Pack vs without, measure week-2 retention.

**Graduation trigger.** Starter Pack raises week-2 retention by ≥20 percentage points. Then it becomes the default onboarding path.

## 5. Public Axiom Library — Opt-In Contributions

**Thesis.** `ontology_axioms` already has a global row (user_id NULL) pattern. The hard version of this is a public library where users can opt in to contribute their highest-confidence axioms, and new users see which axioms have replicated across many records. This is the "Wikipedia of personal truths" and it is the only version of this company that becomes a category-defining platform instead of a premium app. It is also the version most likely to go wrong if built too early.

**Cheapest next experiment.** Add a single toggle in Settings: *"Contribute my axioms anonymously to the global library."* Default off. For the first 100 users, ask explicitly in the onboarding deck. Build a read-only `/library` page showing the top 20 axioms globally, with confidence and replication count.

**Graduation trigger.** ≥30% of paid users opt in within 30 days of enabling the toggle, AND ≥3 axioms have replicated across ≥10 independent user records. At that point, the library moves to the home page.

## 6. AI Assistant Chat — The 24-Hour Advisor

**Thesis.** The Launch doc names this explicitly ("24-hour access to AI… to answer questions and perform tasks on your behalf"). It is currently in the V1 backlog, not V1. The thesis question is not *should we build it* — it is *does it become the primary surface of the product?* If yes, the ontology view is the back room and the chat is the lobby. If no, the chat is a tool and the ontology view stays primary.

**Cheapest next experiment.** Ship a minimal chat tab in the V1.1 release that reads the user's axioms into the system prompt. Measure: do paid users open the chat more than once per session? Do they rate the answers higher than ChatGPT with no ontology?

**Graduation trigger.** ≥60% of weekly-active paid users use chat at least once per session, AND self-reported "this answered my question better than generic AI" ≥ 80%. Then the product reorganizes around the chat.

---

## 7. Importers — Apple Health, MyFitnessPal, Oura

**Thesis.** Every user already has years of data somewhere. Importers collapse the cold-start from "record for four weeks before signal" to "import and see signal in an hour." The risk is that imported data without the user's own language feels generic. The antidote is a one-screen onboarding that turns imported categories into the user's vocabulary before the ontology runs.

**Cheapest next experiment.** Build the Apple Health importer only (largest coverage, cleanest API). Ship it behind a flag for the first 100 users. Measure: do importer-users reach their first axiom faster than non-importer users?

**Graduation trigger.** Importer users reach first axiom ≥2x faster than non-importer users with no drop in axiom confidence. Then every major source gets an importer.
