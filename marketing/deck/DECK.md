## Presenter Notes

**Total time: 10–12 minutes at a calm pace.**

- **Open (slides 1–4, 90s)** — do not rush. Let "19 months" land. Let the room feel the mismatch between data and grammar before you name it.
- **Core (slides 5–6, 60s)** — define "ontology" and "axiom" with the if-then-confidence frame. This is the vocabulary moment. If the room does not have the word, the rest of the deck slides off.
- **Axioms (slides 7–10, 4 min)** — one axiom per minute. On slide 10 (Zero Negative Impact), pause. The 0.95 is the emotional center of the talk. Say, slowly: *"I did not believe this axiom until I saw it."*
- **Meta-insight (slide 11, 45s)** — name vocabulary as the real blocker. Delegation, self-direction, AI — all downstream of this.
- **The product (slides 12–14, 90s)** — describe the three outputs in the order a user feels them, then describe the software plainly.
- **Close (slides 15–16, 60s)** — price lands cleanly on its own slide. Invitation lands on a slide with no number, because the invitation is the number already named on the last slide.

**Rules for delivery:**
- Never read a slide.
- Say the number before you say anything else on that slide.
- One idea per slide, one number per slide, no exceptions.
- If a question lands mid-deck, answer briefly and return to the slide you were on. The deck is a spine, not a conversation.
